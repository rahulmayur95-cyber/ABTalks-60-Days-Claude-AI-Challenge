Recommended Learning Roadmap

Week 1–2
CRM Basics — Free HubSpot Academy Certification
Complete HubSpot's free "CRM Fundamentals" and "Sales Software" certifications (2–4 hours each). Learn pipeline stages, deal tracking, and contact management. Add the certification badge to your LinkedIn immediately.
Week 3–4
Cold Outreach + Lead Generation Fundamentals
Study Aaron Ross's "Predictable Revenue" framework (free summaries available). Practice writing 3 cold email templates for different prospect personas. Watch 5–10 sales call recordings on YouTube (e.g. Sandler Training, JB Sales). Enrol in LinkedIn Sales Navigator free trial.
Month 2
Proposal Writing + Sales Forecasting
Download 3–5 real business proposal templates (Pandadoc or Proposify free resources). Practice building a mock sales forecast model in Excel using pipeline conversion rates. Attend one free online BD workshop via NIIT, IIM Bangalore MOOC, or Coursera audit.
Month 3
Negotiation Skills — Google / Yale Free Course
Complete Yale's "Introduction to Negotiation" on Coursera (free to audit) or Google's Professional Certificate modules. Apply frameworks from your Bikano price benchmarking experience as live negotiation case studies in interviews.
Ongoing
B2B Sales Mindset + Revenue Attribution
Follow sales thought leaders (Justin Welsh, Jeb Blount) on LinkedIn. Set up a personal tracker in Excel to monitor hypothetical pipeline conversion rates. Ask mentors or senior sales colleagues at your next role to shadow calls in the first 30 days.

These are the exact stories and facts to prepare for a BD Executive interview. Structure each answer using the STAR method (Situation → Task → Action → Result).

    AIMA National Finalist — Strategic Decision-Making Under Pressure. Walk through how you qualified from the Regional (where you won 1st place) through the Semifinal to the National Finals at MIT-ADT University, Pune. Describe the business simulation scenarios — revenue targets, competitor moves, live decision-making — and connect it explicitly to how BD executives face similar pressures when closing deals under a monthly target deadline. This is your most powerful differentiator.
    Use this when asked: "What's your biggest achievement?" / "Tell me about a time you worked under pressure" / "What makes you different from other candidates?"
  • Experience
    Bikano Modern Trade Internship — Field Sales & Market Intelligence. Describe how you designed consumer preference surveys across multiple retail outlets, what buying behaviours you found, and how your competitor price benchmarking identified specific market gaps. Quantify where possible: number of outlets covered, competing SKUs compared, frequency of reports submitted. Frame this as your direct experience in translating market intelligence into sales strategy — identical to what a BD executive does for client pipeline decisions.
    Use this when asked: "Do you have sales experience?" / "Tell me about market research you've done" / "How would you approach lead generation?"
  • Initiative
    Digital Marketing KPI Analysis — Tripple One Solutions. Describe your two-month digital marketing internship where you developed cross-platform strategies and analysed engagement and reach KPIs. Highlight the "outstanding performance remarks" you received — be ready to explain what specific tasks earned that recognition (e.g. improving campaign reach, identifying a channel that outperformed others). Connect this to the BD role's expectation of inbound channel management and digital lead generation.
    Use this when asked: "Have you used data to make a business decision?" / "How do you track performance?" / "Tell me about your marketing experience."
  • Project
    Srijan Path — 4P Marketing Strategy for Local Artisans. Describe how you led a 3-month field study identifying the economic challenges of local artisans, developed a tailored 4P (Product, Price, Place, Promotion) marketing strategy, and used AI-supported tools for analysis. This story demonstrates: the ability to understand a client's pain point, develop a go-to-market strategy, and use analytical tools to back your recommendations — precisely the skills a BD executive uses when building client proposals.
    Use this when asked: "Have you developed a strategy from scratch?" / "Tell me about a project you're proud of" / "How do you approach a new market or client segment?"
  • Leadership
    Vice-Head, Cultural Committee, MGKVP. Explain how you overhauled internal reporting processes, streamlined workflows, and reduced turnaround time across key committee functions. Frame this as evidence of process improvement and stakeholder coordination — qualities that are directly relevant to maintaining accurate CRM records and coordinating with sales and marketing teams in a BD role.
    Use this when asked: "Have you held any leadership positions?" / "Tell me about a time you improved a process" / "How do you manage multiple responsibilities?"
  • Global View
    Nusantara Project + Bali ICPM — Cross-Cultural Market Research. Describe your collaboration with an international team studying eco-friendly purchase intentions among Malaysian F&B consumers, and your participation in the Bali International Conference on Cultural Intelligence in Global Management. This directly supports your ability to work with diverse clients and understand consumer behaviour across different cultural and demographic contexts — an edge in B2B client relationship management.

  • Likely Q&A
    Areas Recruiters Are Likely to Probe — Prepare Your Answers. (1) "You don't have direct B2B sales experience — how will you handle targets?" → Answer: Emphasise your Bikano field sales exposure, AIMA simulation, and structured approach to market research, and mention your HubSpot CRM learning plan.
